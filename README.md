<h2><hr><a name="Introduction">Introduction</a></h2>
<a href="https://catalogue.uottawa.ca/search/?P=ELG%204157">GNG 1106</a> Fundamentals of Engineering Computation (3 units).<br><br>
 
Introduction to computer systems. Problem solving for engineering case studies. 
Emphasis is on the design of algorithms and their implementation for solving engineering problems using C. 
.<br><br>
Course Component: Lecture, Labs .<br><br>


Anterequisites: The courses GNG1106, ITI1120, ITI1220, CSI1301, CSI1306, CSI1303, CSI1308, CSI1390 cannot be combined for units.

<hr><h2><a name="labs">Labs</a></h2>

    <ul>
     <li>Lab sessions run weekly, each 170 minutes, starting from Week 2. There will be no lab during the
reading week and the week after</li>
      <li>Each lab session will be hosted by 2 TAs.</li>
      <li>For each lab session, the students need to  <span style="color:red;">need to</span>
	  <ul>
	  <li>complete a pre-lab exercise and submit it 30 minutes before the lab (except Lab 1 where there
is no pre-lab)</li>
     <li>complete the required tasks in the lab, have an interview with your TA, and submit the deliverables
by the end of the lab or, in some cases, by the time by specified by the TA.</li>
	   </ul>
	  </li>
      <li> During the interview, the TA will ask the student for a demonstration and additional questions to
verify the students’ understanding, and decide if the student can submit.</li>
      <li>During the lab, the students may ask the TA for help with other course materials, when the TA is not
busy with helping other students with the designated lab activities.</li>
	  <li>The grading of a lab is based on the student’s performance in the pre-lab, in the interview and in the
submission.</li>
    </ul>
